## The Lab

The Construction Enginering Research Laboratory (CERL) sits on the north side of beautiful, sunny, wonderfully flat, Champaign, IL, a few miles away from the U of I campus.

We have labs spread across a couple buildings, an [earthquake simulator](https://www.erdc.usace.army.mil/Media/Fact-Sheets/Fact-Sheet-Article-View/Article/476734/triaxial-earthquake-and-shock-simulator/), a robotics tent.

## History

CERL opened shop in [1969](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/5e/CERL-_A_history_of_the_U.S._Army_Construction_Engineering_Research_Laboratory_1968-1974_-_USACE-p16021coll4-31.pdf). It started with two labs in the middle of a cornfield.

<div class="img-container">
  <img style="width: 80%" src="/assets/images/old-cerl.png" alt="Old CERL photo">
</div>

It's relationship with UIUC was instrumental in its success. The Army strategically placed CERL next to an elite engineering University so it could enjoy a strong partnership exchanging resources: the two institutions could access each others' labs, the university could share its graduate students, and the university could employ researchers from the lab as professors.

## SMS-TCX

I work within the [Sustaiment Management System](https://www.sms.erdc.dren.mil/) Technical Center of Expertise (SMS-TCX). We are the DoW's gold standard for real property asset management within the Department of War. [BUILDER](https://www.erdc.usace.army.mil/Media/Fact-Sheets/Fact-Sheet-Article-View/Article/476728/builder-sustainment-management-system/) is a web-based software application our team developed for civil engineers and managers to help them decide how to maintain infrastructure. Along with BUILDER, the TCX has produced other software applications including RAILER, ROOFER, and PAVER. As of 2026, these are all rapidly being ingetrated into the new, all-encompassing Enterprise Sustainment Management System (E-SMS).
